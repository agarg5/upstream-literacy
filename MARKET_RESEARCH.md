## What They Do (Inferred from Projects + Founder Background)

Upstream Literacy appears to be building a **network/marketplace platform for K-12 literacy decision-makers** — connecting school district leaders based on demographics and the literacy challenges they face.

**Two Gauntlet projects suggest the product vision:**
1. **Community for Literacy Leaders** — A matching platform where district leaders find peers in similar situations (same district type, size, demographics, challenges). Think "LinkedIn meets matchmaking for district literacy leaders."
2. **E-commerce Shopping Cart** — Likely a storefront for literacy curriculum, professional development materials, or related products.

## Market Position

**The Science of Reading movement is a massive tailwind:**
- Over half of US states have passed laws requiring science-based reading instruction
- What was once "supplemental" literacy tech is now effectively required in classrooms
- Generational buying cycle as districts shift from balanced literacy to phonics/science-of-reading

**Upstream is NOT building another student-facing reading tool.** They're building the B2B network layer:

| Competitor | Focus | Upstream's Differentiation |
|---|---|---|
| Lexia Learning (Cambium/IXL) | AI-powered literacy instruction | Student-facing, not leader network |
| Amira Learning | AI reading tutor | Student-facing |
| 95 Percent Group | Foundational literacy + PD | Product company, not community |
| Istation | Adaptive reading assessment | Student-facing |
| Newsela | Content platform | Student-facing |

**No dominant platform exists** for connecting literacy leaders by district demographics and challenges. This is an underserved niche where peer networks and "what worked in districts like mine" recommendations are enormously valuable.

## Why This Project Matters to Upstream Literacy

**The assignment:** Community platform with search/matching for literacy leaders + e-commerce shopping cart.

**Strategic connection:**

1. **This IS the product.** Unlike other Gauntlet projects that test adjacent skills, this project is literally what Upstream Literacy needs to build. The community matching tool is their core product concept.

2. **AI-powered matching is the value prop.** Connecting district leaders based on both public demographic data (district type, size, free/reduced lunch, ESL population) AND self-selected problem statements requires intelligent matching — semantic similarity, recommendation engines, not just filters.

3. **Single engineer, massive scope.** As a pre-product company with 1 Gauntlet credit, they need ONE engineer who can build the entire stack: community platform, search/matching algorithms, public data ingestion, messaging, moderation tools, and e-commerce.

4. **Speed to market matters.** The Science of Reading legislative wave is creating a time-sensitive window. Getting a community platform live with AI-powered matching is a first-mover advantage.

5. **Founder needs technical execution.** John Hass is a business/strategy leader (Goldman, Rosetta Stone CEO). He knows exactly what the market needs but needs strong technical builders to realize the vision.

## How Your Project Can Stand Out

- Build the matching algorithm well — show semantic understanding of literacy challenges, not just keyword matching
- Ingest real public data sources (NCES, state education data) for district demographics
- Create a thoughtful list of literacy problem statements that shows you understand the domain
- Make the community feel alive — moderation tools, conversation threading, notification system
- Show how AI can surface non-obvious connections between districts ("Districts your size in the Midwest that adopted CKLA saw similar results")
